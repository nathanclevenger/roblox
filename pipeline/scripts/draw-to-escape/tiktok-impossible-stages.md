## Audio Notes
- Music: Intense, driving electronic track with a frustrated energy. Fast tempo, minor key. Percussion is relentless — like a heartbeat that will not slow down. The track builds continuously from [0:00] to [0:24] with no release, creating mounting tension.
  - [0:24-0:27]: The music drops out entirely for the slow-motion launch. Only a rising orchestral swell — strings and brass building from silence to full triumphant blast as the player lands. This is the emotional payoff for 24 seconds of frustration.
  - [0:27-0:30]: Gentle resolution. The music softens to a warm, satisfied hum. Victory earned.
- SFX:
  - Void falls: Silence during the fall, then a distant bass impact on the death screen
  - Staircase expiration: A cascading "paper crumple" sound as each tile dissolves in sequence — fast, like dominoes
  - Lightning: Thunder cracks timed to hit slightly before the visual flash (sound travels faster here for dramatic effect)
  - Wind on The Cliff: Constant howl, mixed prominently — it should feel like the wind is the real enemy
  - The ramp launch at [0:24]: A satisfying "WHOOOOSH" that doppler-shifts as the player flies
  - Summit landing: Heavy, grounded thud — boots on stone — followed by the star chimes
- VO delivery: The narrator is the player. Not a polished voiceover — this should sound like genuine frustration captured from a play session. Unscripted energy. The "YESSSSS" at [0:26] should be raw, unfiltered triumph. The "I dare you" at the end shifts to a calm, knowing tone — they have been through it, and now they are passing the challenge forward.

## Render Notes
- 9:16 vertical framing. The Cliff stages benefit from vertical orientation — the height of the climb fills the frame naturally.
- The death counter in the corner should be visible but not highlighted. Let observant viewers notice it climbing. It tells the story of persistence without narration.
- Speed: Normal speed for all gameplay. The ONLY slow-motion is the ramp launch at [0:24-0:26]. This contrast makes the triumphant moment feel earned.
- Color grade: The Void stages should be extremely dark — the player and their drawings are the only light sources. The Cliff stages should feel harsh and exposed — high contrast, cold blue-white lighting from the storm. The summit victory should shift abruptly to warm golden tones.
- The rapid failure montage at [0:13-0:16] should use jump cuts with no transitions. Each death is a hard cut to the next attempt. The speed sells the frustration.
- Camera work: During the Void stages, the camera should feel isolated — tight on the player with nothing else in frame. On The Cliff, the camera should occasionally tilt down to show the height, reinforcing the stakes. The summit reveal at [0:24-0:27] is the widest shot in the whole video — a dramatic contrast to the claustrophobic failures.
- The trail of failed drawings on the cliff face at [0:27] should be visible but not distracting. They are environmental storytelling — evidence of the journey.
